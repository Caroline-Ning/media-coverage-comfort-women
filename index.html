What Are We Actually Talking about When We Talk about Comfort Women?

An online demo for the data visualizations of a project that investigates the Mainland China media coverage about comfort women (2016-2021).

More

Introduction

This is an online demo for the data visualizations of a corpus-driven project that reviews how Mainland China news represents comfort women in the recent six years. The corpus contains 3195 newspaper articles in Mainland China from 2016 to 2021. Specifically, the project explores why the word "comfort women" was mentioned in each article, the context, and what "comfort women" referred to in the context.

The research finds that the coverage uses the word "comfort women" in both political and humanities contexts. Comfort women can refer to the barrier to Japan-South Korea relations, the war crime, the victims in a documentary, and the victims themselves. Although Mainland China media in this period had a particular focus on the Japan-South Korea relation, the fundamental goal was to criticize Japan's right-wing stance on the comfort women issue, demonstrating China's national discourse. Meanwhile, the national discourse has also contributed to the widespread dissemination of comfort women's stories and the success of relevant documentaries. Additionally, the lack of humanistic focus in the coverage may be due to the limited social activities about comfort women in China's society. Regarding the comparison between central state media and local media, they have some similarities, but local media tend to focus more on the domestic news that has the potential to attract the public.

In a broader view, this project explores the news in the latest six years, points out the changes from the previous years, and furthers the study on the comparison between central state media and local media.

Data & Method

About the Dataset

The corpus of this study contains 3195 newspaper articles from 2016 to 2021. The articles were selected by searching for the word "comfort women" in the database, which means the word "comfort women" can be either in the title or the content.

The data were collected from two databases: People Data and WiseNews of WiseSearch.

People Data is a database based on People's Daily, a governmental unit under the CPC Central Committee. The providers in this database are published mainly by People's Daily (unit). They are categorized as "central news units" in the List of Organizations that Provide Internet News published by the Cyberspace Administration of China. The other providers in People Data are incorporated based on the database's close corporation with the General Office of the CPC Central Committee, the Central Party School, and other central ministries.

WiseNews is a news archive with items from more than 1600 providers from Mainland China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and some Asia Pacific countries. Most providers in WiseNews are mostly local media in Mainland China. The two groups' overlapping source is Global Times, a central state media, so it was removed from the WiseNews group.

Methodology

The basic methodology is to tag each article with its topic, categorizing every article. For instance, for the most notable category, "Japan-South Korea Disagreement," the article was tagged as such because the article is about conflicts between Japan and Korea, such as the controversy about removing the comfort women statue in Seoul and the international trade conflicts.

The tagging is completed manually by reading each article. The categories are generated dynamically along with reading the articles, which means that if the article cannot be categorized, there will be a new category. If two categories have a lot of overlapping themes, they will be combined as one. There are 49 categories in the end.

Additionally, this research hopes to develop an analysis of the similarities and differences between the central state media and local media. With the background of the two databases, People Data becomes a source for central state media as People's Daily is one of the most influential newspapers owned by the CCP. WiseSearch became a source for local media. Two media sources (China News and Liberation Daily) in WiseNews belong to central state media, so they were put into the People Data group.

technical intervention

Articles are parsed by a chrome extension, Web Scraper, and Python. Each article was recorded with title, source, date, and content. The data are stored in Excel, and the data visualization graphs are designed in Tableau. As a support, a timeline is created by timeline.js for the events mentioned more frequently each year in the corpus to help provide a more comprehensive introduction to the topic. This project also explores the distant reading of the corpus by a tokenization tool, Mini Word Cloud, for the Chinese language, which leads to the word frequency of the top 900 words in the corpus. The word frequencies help generate a word cloud for the corpus.
