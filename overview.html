Overview

Article count, News Countries & Relations, Mentioned Leaders, Word Cloud

01

The graph shows the article count of the two media groups and their sum change over the years, from 2016 to 2021. The article count reaches its peak year in 2017, significantly drops in 2018, and remains low from 2019 to 2021. Among the 3195 articles, 1723 are from the central state media group, and 1472 are from the local media group. Thus, it is expected that in most years, the state media group has more articles, but in 2017, the local media group has more articles. 

The peak year of the article count, 2017, was a special year for Korea. Park Geun-hye was impeached in December 2016, and Korea's new president Moon Jae-in took office in May 2017. The two presidents held opposite attitudes toward an agreement that had previously been reached about comfort women, the Japan–South Korea Comfort Women Agreement confirmed by Park Geun-hye's government on December 28th, 2015.

2017 was also a special year for comfort women-related events in China. Twenty-two, a documentary about comfort women, was released in 2017. 2017 was also the 80th year after the outbreak of the second Sino-Japanese War and the 80th year after the Nanjing Massacre, so memorial news reports may have increased. Additionally, Chinese tourists in Japan found books denying the Nanjing Massacre in a hotel operated by a Japanese company , which leads to much criticism of the Japanese right-wing force in Chinese media..

The trough year of the article count is 2020. Due to the coronavirus outbreak, the media may have paid less attention to other topics than the pandemic. From 2019 to 2021, the media did not show as much interest as in the previous years.

02

Most of the articles in the corpus are international news rather than domestic news. This graph is based on the tag of each article and displays what countries or regions the media focus on. The news about China and the relations between China and other countries act out a small role (less than 20%). This pattern illustrates that "comfort women" is considered an international issue in Mainland China media. It also shows that the most notable countries or regions engaged with the topic are Japan, South Korea, China, Taiwan, and the US.

03

This graph compares the word frequency of the five countries' or regions' (Japan, South Korea, China, Taiwan, and the US) leaders' names and their days in office from 2016 to 2021. I added the y-axis of days in office because a critical factor of the leader's word frequency is his or her days in office in the period 2016 to 2021. The result shows the media attention towards the leaders in the articles relevant to comfort women. For instance, although Park Geun-hye's days in office were relatively short, she is more frequently mentioned in the corpus. She has attracted the most media attention, probably because of her government's decision on the 2015 Japan–South Korea Comfort Women Agreement and her political scandal in 2016.

04

The word cloud uses a human shape with words to illustrate "this is how words construct comfort women." It provides an immediate impression of words that dominate the corpus: Abe, Korea, issue, China, country, history, comfort women, and Japan. In one sentence, the coverage emphasizes that "comfort women are regarded as a historical issue in Korea, China, and Japan."
